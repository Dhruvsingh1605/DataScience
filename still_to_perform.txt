❌ 1. Free-form consulting or open-ended advice
Example:

"What game should I invest in?"

"Tell me about trends in racing games."

"Why are JRPGs declining?"

🧠 Why it fails:

No clear action/target/filters.

These are opinion-based or require multi-hop reasoning and/or temporal context.

Gemini will either hallucinate JSON or output irrelevant structure.

✅ Fix: Route such queries to a consulting module (LLM-based answer generation or retrieval QA).

❌ 2. Predictive / Forecasting questions
Example:

"Will action games sell more next year?"

"Predict sales in 2026 for Nintendo games."

🧠 Why it fails:

No "future" data in tabular dataset.

Requires time-series or ML forecasting model — which JSON query structure cannot express.

✅ Fix: Route to a forecasting engine (e.g., Prophet, LSTM, ARIMA, or sklearn pipeline).

❌ 3. Logical or comparative reasoning
Example:

"Are sports games more popular than racing games?"

"Which platform grew the most over the last decade?"

"Did Nintendo outperform Sony in the EU market?"

🧠 Why it fails:

Needs comparison across filtered groups, multi-stage aggregation, or delta calculations.

Gemini may give you one valid filter/group_by, but not both sides of comparison.

✅ Fix: Use a reasoning layer that computes multiple queries and compares them post hoc.

❌ 4. Ambiguous or underspecified questions
Example:

"Show me top games."

"Best-selling titles?"

"How is sales going?"

🧠 Why it fails:

Lacks filters, timeframe, region — the parser might default to global sales, all-time, no filter… or guess incorrectly.

✅ Fix: Add a query clarification layer or ask Gemini to request clarification if ambiguity is detected.

❌ 5. Non-column-based questions
Example:

"What does the 'Other_Sales' column mean?"

"Who created this dataset?"

"How many missing values are there?"

🧠 Why it fails:

These are metadata, documentation, or data-quality questions — not analytical queries.

✅ Fix: Route to a data-docs assistant or schema documentation service.

❌ 6. Multistep / multi-action queries
Example:

"Show me the average global sales of action games, then compare it with strategy games."

"Give me top 5 genres in 2009 and their total sales."

🧠 Why it fails:

One action per JSON output: can't express sequence or composition.

You’ll get either action A or B — not both.

✅ Fix: Use a multi-query planner that chains or splits the input intelligently.

❌ 7. Queries involving external knowledge
Example:

"Did FIFA outsell Call of Duty in the World Cup year?"

"Was 2008 a recession year and did it affect game sales?"

🧠 Why it fails:

These questions mix internal data with external world knowledge.

Your system has no access to real-world events or trends.

✅ Fix: Integrate with a knowledge graph or external facts LLM.
